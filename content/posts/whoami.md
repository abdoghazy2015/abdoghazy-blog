## `$ whoami`

Hey there, I'm Abdelhameed Ghazy. 

I'm a 27-year-old from Egypt who spends way too much time staring at terminals, digging into the internals of how things work—and more importantly, how to break them. I graduated with a degree in Computer Engineering from Al-Ahram Canadian University, but my real education started the moment I fell down the cybersecurity rabbit hole.

## The Technical Side

My main focus is offensive security research. I'm deeply passionate about how systems operate at a fundamental level, zoning in specifically on **Web Security** and **Pwn** (binary exploitation/reverse engineering). There is a specific kind of adrenaline rush that comes from patch diffing, writing a clean exploit, or finally popping a shell after hours of analyzing a tough target. It’s that exact drive that led me to found CyEagle and push the boundaries of automated penetration testing at CyNerv.

I'm also heavily involved in the competitive side of the industry. I play CTFs actively and have the privilege of being the captain of the **0xL4ugh** team. Leading a top-tier CTF team means a lot of sleepless weekends, coordinating solvers, and developing challenges, but the community and the constant learning curve make it completely worth it.

## Away from the Keyboard

I try to make sure I don't let the screen completely consume my life. When I'm stepping away from the laptop and taking a break from vulnerability hunting, you can usually find me at the gym training MMA. I've found that stepping onto the mat requires the exact same discipline, focus, and real-time problem-solving as a complex pwn challenge—just with a bit more physical impact.

I started this blog to document my research, share writeups, and give back to the community that taught me so much. 

Feel free to reach out if you want to talk about exploit dev, web vulnerabilities, or just swap CTF war stories.
